Dataset 1: company_data
Description of Columns:

company_id: Unique identifier for the company.
company_name: Name of the company.
Industry: Industry to which the company belongs (e.g., Technology, Finance, Healthcare). 
Country: Country where the company is headquartered. 
founded_year: The year the company was founded.
market_cap: Market capitalization of the company (in billions USD).

Dataset 2: stock_price_data
Description of Columns:

price_id: Unique identifier for the stock price record.
company_id: Unique identifier for the company (linked to company_data).
Date: The date of the stock price record. 
open_price: Opening price of the stock on that date.
close_price: Closing price of the stock on that date.
high_price: Highest price of the stock during that day.
low_price: Lowest price of the stock during that day.
Volume: The trading volume of the stock on that date. 

Dataset 3: trading_volume_data
Description of Columns:

volume_id: Unique identifier for the trading volume record.
company_id: Unique identifier for the company.
Date: Date of the trading activity. 
buy_volume: The number of shares bought.
sell_volume: The number of shares sold.
net_volume: The difference between buy and sell volumes.

Dataset 4: financial_transaction_data
Description of Columns:

transaction_id: Unique identifier for the financial transaction.
company_id: Unique identifier for the company.
transaction_date: The date of the transaction.
transaction_type: Type of transaction (e.g., Buy, Sell, Dividend, Split).
transaction_amount: Total amount involved in the transaction (in USD).
transaction_currency: Currency in which the transaction was conducted (e.g., USD, EUR).

Dataset 5: company_performance_data
Description of Columns:

performance_id: Unique identifier for the performance record.
company_id: Unique identifier for the company.
Date: The date of the performance record. 
Revenue: Revenue of the company for that quarter/year (in millions USD). 
Profit: Profit of the company for that quarter/year (in millions USD). 
Eps: Earnings per share (EPS) for the company. 

Explanation of the Datasets:
Company Data: Contains 500 records of company details, including industry, market capitalization, and location.
Stock Price Data: Tracks daily stock prices (open, close, high, low) and trading volumes over 10,000 records.
Trading Volume Data: Contains 5,000 records of daily buy and sell volumes for each company, along with the net volume (buy - sell).
Financial Transaction Data: Includes 8,000 records of transactions like stock buys, sells, dividends, and splits, along with transaction amounts and currencies.
Company Performance Data: Contains 3,000 records of company performance metrics such as revenue, profit, and earnings per share (EPS).
